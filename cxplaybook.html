AI Agents in Customer Support:
How to Prove the ROI to Your CFO

A field guide for CS and AE teams. Use before and after deployment to quantify and communicate business value across three dimensions: quality, volume, and resource intensity.

Function: Customer Support Deployment stage: Pre & post Typical ROI window: 60–90 days
01 What Agents Can You Build — and What Do They Change?

In a customer support function, AI agents address three types of operational problems. Each maps to a distinct improvement dimension and produces measurable outcomes.

Quality improvement
Volume improvement
Resource intensity improvement
Quality improvement
Triage & routing agent

Reads incoming tickets, classifies intent and urgency, and routes to the right team or agent automatically — with consistent logic applied every time.

Quality Fewer mis-routes, faster first response, higher CSAT. Removes human inconsistency from the first touchpoint.
Quality improvement
Response drafting agent

Drafts a reply for every incoming ticket using context from the conversation history, knowledge base, and customer record. The agent reviews and sends.

Quality Consistent tone and accuracy at scale. Reduces agent cognitive load on routine replies, freeing attention for nuanced cases.
Volume improvement
Tier-1 resolution agent

Handles FAQs, password resets, billing inquiries, and other high-frequency low-complexity tickets end-to-end — no human in the loop.

Volume 30–60% of ticket volume resolved autonomously. Human team focuses exclusively on cases that require judgment or empathy.
Volume improvement
Proactive outreach agent

Monitors product usage signals and triggers personalised outreach when a customer shows signs of confusion or disengagement — before they file a ticket.

Volume Deflects inbound volume at the source. Reduces tickets created rather than just tickets handled.
Resource intensity improvement
Post-interaction summarisation & CRM update agent

Automatically summarises every resolved conversation and updates the CRM record — eliminating the manual wrap-up work agents do after each ticket (typically 2–4 minutes per interaction).

Resource Reduces average handle time by 20–30% without changing the resolution itself. The same team handles more volume, or the same volume with less fatigue.
How to use this with a prospect

Ask: "Where does your team lose the most time today — handling tickets, routing them, or updating records afterwards?" The answer tells you which agent type to lead with in the business case.

02 Baseline Discovery: What to Capture Before Deployment

ROI is a delta. These are the five inputs you need to calculate it. If the customer doesn't have exact figures, use the industry defaults provided.

Question to ask Default if unknown
1
Monthly ticket volume — and is it growing?
50–200 tickets/employee/month (B2B SaaS)
2
Average handle time per ticket (incl. wrap-up)
8–12 min AHT; 2–4 min wrap-up
3
Support team size and fully-loaded cost per agent
$75–90k fully-loaded, mid-market
4
Current CSAT or NPS — and churn rate
5-pt CSAT gain ≈ 1–2% churn reduction (SaaS)
5
Share of tickets that are repetitive / tier-1
40–70% of B2B SaaS tickets are automatable
